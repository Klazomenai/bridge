# Stewardship of Bridge

This document sets out the commitments **Klazomenai** makes to contributors
and users of Bridge. It complements [LICENSE.md](LICENSE.md) and
[CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md) by stating, in plain words, what we
*will* and *will not* do with the work that lands in this repository.

It is a public commitment, not a legal contract. It binds Klazomenai by
reputation rather than by enforcement clause — but reputation, on the open
seas, is no small thing.

## ⚓ The Promise

### 1. The current open-source release stays open

Any version of Bridge published under an OSI-approved open-source
licence — at the time the contributions were accepted — will remain
available under that licence, **in perpetuity**.

We do not retroactively close down published releases. The version of
the codebase that includes your contribution today, under today's
licence, will still be available tomorrow under today's licence. Forever.

### 2. Relicensing is OSI-bounded

The Contributor Licence Agreement grants Klazomenai the right to relicense
*future development* under any licence approved by the
[Open Source Initiative](https://opensource.org/licenses) — but **not** under
proprietary terms, source-available terms, or any licence the OSI has not
blessed.

If we ever wished to move outside that boundary — to take Bridge
proprietary, to introduce a Business Source License or similar — we
would need fresh consent from contributors. The CLA does not grant that
consent in advance, and we will not interpret it as if it did.

### 3. Forks are honoured

If Klazomenai ever changes direction in a way the community disagrees
with, the existing open-source release of Bridge remains forkable under
its original licence. We will not pursue trademark claims, contractual
claims, or any other mechanism to obstruct a good-faith fork of an
open-source release.

The work belongs to the people who wrote it. We are stewards, not owners.

### 4. Decisions are visible

Material licensing decisions are discussed in public issues *before*
they are made. The 🪝 *"Holler from the Crow's Nest"* section in our
licensing-related issues exists for that reason. There is no quiet
relicensing on this fleet.

If you ever find yourself reading release notes and wondering "wait,
when did that get decided?" — that's a stewardship failure. Tell us.

## 🤧 Why this document exists

The Contributor Licence Agreement's legal language is, of necessity,
abstract. This document is the plain-English version of intent.

A pattern of licence changes over the past decade — MongoDB → SSPL
(2018), Elastic → SSPL+ELv2 (2021), Sentry → BUSL/FSL (2019/2023),
Redis → RSALv2+SSPL (2024) — has eroded contributor trust in CLAs. Each
of those changes was *legally* enabled by a CLA contributors had signed
under earlier, more permissive terms. Each was experienced as a
betrayal by some part of the community. Each produced a fork.

We don't want any of that on this fleet. We want the stance legible up
front, and we want to be held to it.

## 🦜 What this means for you

You can contribute knowing:

- Your code, accepted under AGPL-3.0-or-later, will always be available
  under AGPL-3.0-or-later — no future relicensing affects the release
  that included your contribution.
- Future development might move between OSI-approved licences (for
  example AGPL-3.0-or-later → MPL-2.0). It will not move to proprietary
  or source-available terms.
- Your work will not be quietly conscripted into a closed-source product.
- If something ever changes, you'll see it discussed in public first.

## 📜 When this document is updated

If this document is ever amended in a way that *weakens* the commitments
above, that change must:

1. Be discussed in a public issue at least **28 days** before it takes
   effect.
2. Be merged via a pull request that any contributor can comment on.
3. Be announced in release notes for the next version after the change.

Tightening or clarifying the commitments — for example, adding new
promises on top of existing ones — does not require this notice period.
